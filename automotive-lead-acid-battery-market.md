<p><p>The automotive lead-acid battery market primarily consists of two types: VRLA (Valve-Regulated Lead-Acid) batteries and flooded batteries. VRLA batteries are sealed, maintenance-free, and can operate in various orientations, making them ideal for modern vehicles and equipment. Flooded batteries, on the other hand, require regular maintenance, including monitoring electrolyte levels, and are typically used in applications where cost is more critical than convenience. Both types serve essential roles in starting, powering, and lighting vehicles.</p></p>
