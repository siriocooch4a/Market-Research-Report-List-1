<p><p>An Avalanche Photodiode (APD) is a highly sensitive semiconductor device that converts light into electrical current. It operates by using the avalanche effect, where a small amount of incident light generates electron-hole pairs, leading to multiplication of the charge carriers and resulting in an amplified current signal. APDs are widely used in applications such as telecommunications, medical devices, and industrial sensing due to their high speed and sensitivity.</p><p>The APD market is projected to experience significant growth, driven by advancements in technology and increasing demand for high-performance optical communication systems.
