<p><p>The lithium-ion battery materials market comprises several key components. Cathode materials, often lithium cobalt oxide or lithium iron phosphate, determine energy capacity and stability. Anode materials, primarily graphite, facilitate charge retention. Lithium-ion battery separators enable ion flow while preventing short circuits. Electrolytes, usually organic solvents with lithium salts, allow the movement of lithium ions between the electrodes. Together, these materials are crucial for enhancing battery performance, energy density, and safety, driving advancements in various applications, particularly electric vehicles and portable electronics.</p></p>
